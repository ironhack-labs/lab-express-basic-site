Antonio Francesco Gramsci (UK: /ˈɡræmʃi/,[4] US: /ˈɡrɑːmʃi/;[5] Italian: [anˈtɔːnjo franˈtʃesko ˈɡramʃi] (About this soundlisten); 22 January 1891 – 27 April 1937) was an Italian Marxist philosopher, journalist, linguist, writer and politician. He wrote on philosophy, political theory, sociology, history and linguistics. He was a founding member and one-time leader of the Communist Party of Italy and was imprisoned by Benito Mussolini's Fascist regime.
