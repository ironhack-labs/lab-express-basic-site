The Saint

First Years

Born in the city of Oriente-SP, on August 4, 1973, Marcos started football at Lençoense, a team from the municipality of Lençóis Paulista, in the interior of the state of São Paulo. He remained at Lençoense from 1990 to 1992, when he was hired by Palmeiras. Shortly before turning 19, still in 1992, he debuted on the professional team of Palmeiras in a friendly match against Esportiva Guaratinguetá. Palmeiras won the duel by 4 to 0. However, Marcos's first official match took place only in 1996, against Botafogo-SP, a game also won by the score of 4 to 0 by the Palestra Itália team, with the right to a penalty saved by archer.

Glory moments

After spending a lot of time in Velloso's reserve, Marcos was established as a starter only in 1999, in the Copa Libertadores de América. It was in the quarter-finals of the tournament that the goalkeeper was consecrated: in the penalty shootout against Corinthians, he defended the collection of Vampeta and guaranteed his team in the next phase of the competition. In the semifinal, Palmeiras eliminated River Plate-ARG, after being defeated 1-0 in Buenos Aires, and winning 3-0 in São Paulo. In the final, after being defeated away from home by Deportivo Cali-COL and having won at Palestra Itália, the decision went to penalties. Marcos proved to have a star, since he did not defend, but he saw Bedoya and Zapata waste their charges and Alviverde won the Libertadores da América for the first time in their history. Marcos receive the prize of beste player of the tournament (despite having played only the last 6 matches). Since then he was nicknamed Saint Marcos.

The failure

The title accredited Palmeiras to play the World Club Championship at the end of that season, in Japan. The match was against Manchester United, from England, and a failure by Marcos was decisive for Palmeiras not to win the trophy. After a cross made by Ryan Giggs, the goalkeeper was unable to intercept the ball, which was left to Roy Keane to score the only goal of the duel.

The comeback

The following year, fate again put arch-rivals Palmeiras and Corinthians face to face in the South American tournament, only this time in the semifinals. After two electrifying duels, once again the teams ended on an equal footing and the spot in the final was decided on penalties. Everyone had converted their charges, until Marcelinho Carioca, one of Corinthians's historic idols, charged in the right corner of Marcos, who flew to make the defense and take Palmeiras to the final of the competition. São Paulo city became green again!

The outcome of the tournament was not the happiest for the archer of Palmeiras. Against Boca Juniors, again the decision went to penalties, however, Marcos' star did not shine and the Argentine team won the cup.

World champion

Marcos was summoned by Luiz Felipe Scolari for the 2002 World Cup. The goalkeeper made very important saves during the tournament, especially in the final, against Germany, helping to guarantee Brazil's fifth world championship. From then on, the archer won the sympathy of the entire Brazilian population and became a national sport idol.

Last years - second division and the settlement as a legend

In the same year of consecration, Marcos saw his team relegated to Serie B of the Brazilian Championship. Soon after, the player received a proposal to play for Arsenal, from England, but his passion for Palmeiras did not allow him to complete the transfer, remaining in Parque Antártica and helping to bring Palmeiras back to the first division of the National championship.

In the following years, Palmeiras's "lean cows" time also affected Marcos' performance, who suffered from repeated injuries. One of the most serious was in March 2007, against Juventus, when the injury took away the archer of that season.

Marcos' comeback occured in 2008. After a long fast, the goalkeeper again lifted a cup with Palmeiras, in the Paulista Championship, after beating Ponte Preta 5-0 in the decisive match, played at Palestra Itália. This was the last title won by Marcos as a professional soccer player.
